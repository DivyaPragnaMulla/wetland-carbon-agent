# Wetland Carbon Monitoring — Domain Knowledge

## Core Science
- Wetlands shift from carbon SINK to SOURCE when water table drops below -20cm
- IPCC critical threshold: >2°C temperature anomaly above baseline
- WHO PM2.5 annual limit: 15 μg/m³ | 24hr limit: 45 μg/m³
- Positive CO₂ flux = carbon source | Negative CO₂ flux = carbon sink
- EU Water Framework Directive requires good ecological status

## Risk Thresholds
| Parameter | Low | Moderate | High | Critical |
|-----------|-----|----------|------|----------|
| Water Table | >-5cm | -5 to -10cm | -10 to -20cm | <-20cm |
| Temp Anomaly | <1°C | 1-1.5°C | 1.5-2°C | >2°C |
| CO₂ Flux | <0 | 0-1 | 1-2 | >2 μmol/m²/s |

## Data Sources
- FluxNet: global carbon flux open datasets
- LifeWatch Italy: EU ecological monitoring
- OpenAQ: live air quality measurements
- ERA5: historical climate reanalysis data
- NASA ORNL DAAC: ecosystem carbon data

## Intervention Guidelines
- CRITICAL: Immediate water table restoration, daily monitoring
- HIGH: Weekly monitoring, prepare intervention plan
- MODERATE: Bi-weekly monitoring, seasonal review
- LOW: Monthly monitoring, routine checks
